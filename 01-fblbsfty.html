FabLab Safety

Why is safety so important in every workshop?

  • To ensure all users have safety education and training
  • To create a safe environment with ZERO incidents/ injuries

3-step procedure to follow in case of emergency:

  • For ambulance or fire brigade, call 995
  • Instruct them to enter Gate 1 (Main Entrance) along Dover Road
  • Inform Campus Security by calling 6772-1234 for all emergencies and incident reporting.

Common rules to remember:

  • Always wear long pants and covered shoes into any lab/ workshop
  • Always abide by PPE requirements as can be seen on the tag on every machine
  • Tie up all loose articles (necklaces, loose bangles, etc) and hair
  • Never wear gloves when using any sort of drill (entanglement hazard)
  • Never operate machinery when under drowsy medication/ alcohol or when you feel that you are unable to focus

A list of all PPE and warning signs used in the FABlab:

According to the SP intro to fablab PDF, these are the types of hazards due to machinery.

  • Entanglement hazards
  • Cutting hazards
  • Impact hazards
  • Shearing hazards
  • Crushing hazards
  • Draw-in hazards
  • Friction and abrasion hazards

Always remember that it only takes a small mistake for an accident to happen
